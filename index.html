About

The story so far

My Journey

I was born in Rawalpindi, Pakistan — a bustling garrison city nestled against the Margalla Hills, where curiosity about the natural world came early and stayed. For my undergraduate studies, I moved to Denver, Colorado, where the Rocky Mountain landscape felt a world away from home but the drive to understand biology at its deepest level only grew stronger. At Metropolitan State University of Denver, I earned my B.S. in Biology with a minor in Chemistry, and it was there that I got my first real taste of research — studying how Neisseria lactamica influences oral biofilm formation and developing electrochemical biosensors for pathogenic Mycoplasma detection.

Those early projects taught me that the most fascinating questions in biology sit at the molecular scale, where a single protein can determine whether a tissue stays healthy or spirals toward disease. That realization brought me to the University of Kansas in 2020, where I joined Prof. Kristi Neufeld's laboratory as a PhD student. Here, I've been able to go deep into the questions that drive me — how the tumor suppressor APC maintains intestinal homeostasis, what role its nuclear localization plays in regulating inflammation, and how the gut microbiome intersects with these pathways in sex-specific ways. My days involve cell culture, immunoblotting, histology, mouse colony management, and piecing together the molecular puzzle of colon biology.

Along the way, I've presented at national conferences including the AACR Annual Meeting, mentored undergraduate and graduate students, contributed to grant proposals, and developed a deep appreciation for the collaborative nature of biomedical research. With my defense scheduled for April 2026, I'm eager to apply my expertise in molecular biology and DNA repair to the next chapter of my scientific career.

Beyond the Lab

When I'm not running Western blots or sectioning tissue at the microtome, you might find me reflecting on the journey from Rawalpindi to Lawrence, Kansas — two very different worlds that taught me resilience and adaptability. My time volunteering in the NICU at North Suburban Medical Center showed me the human side of science, and it continues to inspire my work at the bench. I believe the best research comes from a place of genuine care for the people it ultimately serves.

Research

Current investigations

01

Nuclear APC & Colonic Inflammation

Neufeld Lab · KU · 2020 – Present

The tumor suppressor APC is best known for its role in Wnt signaling, but its nuclear functions remain incompletely understood. My research investigates how nuclear localization of APC couples epithelial NF-κB control to mucin homeostasis and shapes sex-specific inflammatory outcomes in the colon. Using mammalian cell culture, immunoblotting, immunofluorescence, and mouse models, I've uncovered pathways linking nuclear APC to the regulation of inflammatory chemokines and neutrophil recruitment.

02

Mucus Barrier Integrity & MUC2

Neufeld Lab · KU · 2020 – Present

The colonic mucus barrier is a critical first line of defense against luminal pathogens and inflammatory insults. I investigate how nuclear APC regulates MUC2 expression — the primary gel-forming mucin — and what happens to barrier integrity when this regulation breaks down. Using histological techniques including Alcian Blue staining, immunohistochemistry, and fluorescence in situ hybridization, I characterize changes in mucin production and goblet cell biology across different genetic backgrounds.

03

Microbiome & Helicobacter-Mediated Protection

Neufeld Lab · KU · 2020 – Present

The gut microbiome can either exacerbate or protect against colitis, and the mechanisms that tip this balance are poorly understood. I'm studying how Helicobacter hepaticus colonization drives APC-dependent microbiome remodeling that paradoxically protects against colitis. Using 16S rRNA sequencing, PICRUSt functional analysis, and gnotobiotic mouse approaches, I aim to identify which microbial communities and metabolic pathways are reshaped by nuclear APC status.
